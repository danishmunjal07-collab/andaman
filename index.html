Andaman & Nicobar Islands

About

Andaman & Nicobar Islands are famous for beautiful beaches, coral reefs, forests and marine life.

Radhanagar Beach

Radhanagar Beach, located on Swaraj Dweep (formerly Havelock Island) in the Andaman & Nicobar Islands, is celebrated as one of Asia's finest beaches. Famous for its pristine white sand, calm turquoise waters, and lush tropical backdrop, this award-winning, eco-friendly Blue Flag beach is the ultimate spot for swimming and watching spectacular sunsets.

Cellular Jail

The Cellular Jail, also known as Kala Pani (Black Water), is a colonial-era prison in Port Blair, Andaman and Nicobar Islands, India. Built by the British in 1906, the complex was used to exile and subject Indian independence activists to solitary confinement and inhumane torture. Today, it stands as a revered national memorial.

Ross Island

Ross Island, officially named Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose Island, is a tiny but historically rich island in the Andaman archipelago, located just 3 km east of Port Blair. It is famous for its British colonial ruins, abundance of free-roaming wildlife.

Marine Life

The Andaman Sea is renowned for its incredible biodiversity, housing over 1,200 fish species, vibrant coral reefs, and rare marine mammals. Key wildlife includes the elusive Dugong (the state animal of the Andaman Islands), diverse reef sharks, green sea turtles, and massive manta rays.Enjoy snorkelling, scuba diving and many other water sports

Eco-Friendly Tips

  • Do not throw plastic.
  • Keep beaches clean.
  • Protect coral reefs.
  • Use reusable bottles.
